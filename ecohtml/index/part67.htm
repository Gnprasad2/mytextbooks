M3 = M1 + Net time deposits of public with the banks

Some economists believe that time deposits should be included in the money supply as they are an important form of store of value as in the case of fixed or timed deposits the depositors can borrow from the banks against them. Also, in some cases the depositors are allowed to withdraw their deposits after foregoing some interest and paying a penalty. It is also known as broad money as it included wider definition of money. The basic difference between M1 and M3 is the treatment of timed deposits with the banks. Narrow money excludes the timed deposits of the public with the banking system on the ground that they are incoming earning assets and not liquid in the real sense.

M3 is the most commonly used measure of money supply. It is also known as aggregate monetary resources
