Oceanic Transport

➢The wide range of ocean transport is rendered possible by the fundamental geographical fact that the world’s ocean and seas are interconnected, whereas the land areas are disconnected.

➢Cheapness of water transport is due to a number of factors:

º It is not necessary to construct a route, except for the dredging of harbour channels

º Sea transport involves less power than by rail or road transport.

º Transport costs are distributed over large loads, thus lowering the cost per tonne, and

º At comparatively low speeds, friction is less on sea than on land
