Origin of Peninsular Drainage

º Subsidence of western flank of peninsula during early tertiary period. It his disturbed the symmetry of peninsular block’s river watershed.

º Upheaval of the Himalayas when the northern flank of peninsular block was subjected to subsidence and consequent trough faulting. The Narmada and Tapi flow in trough faults and fill the original cracks with detritus materials. Hence there is lack of alluvial and deltaic deposits in these rivers.

º Slight tilting of peninsular block from North-West to South-East has caused entire drainage system to flow towards Bay of Bengal.
